Scandinavian Countries

Scandinavia is a geography formed by countries in Northern Europe. Denmark, Sweden and Norway are Scandinavian countries. Together with these countries, Finland, Iceland, Faroe Islands, Åland Islands and Greenland form the Nordic countries. The languages ​​of these countries are similar to each other except Finnish.

COUNTRIES

Denmark

Denmark, or officially the Kingdom of Denmark, is a Scandinavian country in Northern Europe. Most of Denmark, located in the southernmost of the Scandinavian countries, is on the Jylland peninsula. The capital is built on Sjælland, the largest of the Danish islands.

Capital City: Copenhagen

Country Code: +45

Space: 42.933 km²

Population: 5,806 million (2019) European Statistical Office

Currency: Danish Krone

Queen: II. Margrethe

Official language: Danish

Norway

Norway, or officially the Kingdom of Norway, is a country in the western part of the Scandinavian Peninsula in Northern Europe. Neighboring Finland, Sweden and Russia, the country has a coast on the Norwegian Sea, an arm of the Atlantic Ocean, to the west. Its shores are lined by thousands of fjords.

Capital: Oslo

Population: 5,328 million (2019) European Statistical Office

Currency: Norwegian Krone

Prime Minister: Erna Solberg

Official language: Norwegian

Sweden

Sweden, or officially the Kingdom of Sweden, is a country located on the Scandinavian peninsula in Northern Europe. The country's border neighbors are Norway to the west and north, and Finland to the east. Sweden is also connected to Denmark with the Öresund Bridge in the south.

Capital: Stockholm

Country code: +46

Currency: Swedish Krona

Population: 10.23 million (2019) European Statistical Office

Official language: Swedish

Finland

Finland, officially the Republic of Finland, is a Scandinavian country in Northern Europe. It borders Russia to the east, Norway to the north, and Sweden to the west. It has coasts on the Gulf of Botnia in the west and the Gulf of Finland in the south. Its area is 338,455 km² and its population is 5.5 million. Helsinki is the country's capital and largest city.

Capital: Helsinki

Population: 5,518 million (2019) European Statistical Office

Currency: Euro

Prime Minister: Sanna Marin

President: Sauli Niinistö

Official languages: Finnish, Swedish

Iceland

Iceland is an island and European country located in the north of the Atlantic Ocean, southeast of Greenland and northwest of Scandinavia and Great Britain. Iceland is surrounded by the Arctic Ocean to the north. With a population of 356,991 and an area of ​​103,000 km², it is the most sparsely populated country in Europe.

Capital: Reykjavik

Area: 103,000 km²

Population: 356. 991 (2019) European Statistical Office

Currency: Icelandic Krona

Chairman: Guðni Th. Johannesson
